# Agent Prompt — Level 7 to Level 20 Design: Jungle Home, Tracking Trails, Dog Companion, Wildlife Chase, and Human Encounters

## Main Objective

Design and implement Levels **7 to 20** as the next major phase of **Jungle Escape: Lost Path**.

After Level 5 and Level 6, the player enters the **Wildlands of Peace**, learns about living alongside animals, unlocks Sand Shoes, and begins preparing to build a small jungle home/base.

From Level 7 onward, the game must feel like:

* a jungle survival expedition
* a treasure hunt
* a home-building journey
* a wildlife tracking adventure
* a growing relationship with nature, animals, and people

The game should no longer feel like simple straight running levels.

---

# Important Wildlife Rule

The game must avoid graphic hunting or animal harm.

Animal chase levels should be designed as:

* tracking animals
* following footprints
* rescuing animals
* photographing animals
* tagging animals safely for research
* collecting trail clues
* learning animal behavior
* protecting wildlife paths

Do not create gameplay where the player kills animals.

The tone should be:

```text
Respect wildlife. Track carefully. Learn from nature. Do not harm animals.
```

## Chapter 3 — The Wildlands Settlement

After crossing the sandy path, Kairo and Zuri discover a peaceful clearing near the jungle edge. It has enough space to become their first expedition home.

The player must collect building materials, unlock a tracking dog companion, follow animal trails, meet local characters, and slowly build a safe jungle home/base.

This chapter introduces:

* jungle home construction
* land ownership progression
* animal tracking trails
* dog companion system
* animal chase/tracking missions
* human NPC encounters
* new collectibles
* new resources

* new map zones

# New Core Systems

## 1. Jungle Home Building

The player collects materials while running.

Resources include:

```text
coins
gems
bricks
tiles
windows
wood
food
tools
rope
water
medicine_pack
map_pieces
sunstone_shards
animal_badges
```

The player uses these to build their jungle home.

## 2. Tracking Trails

Replace boring straight tracks and water-paddle layouts with **wildlife tracking paths**.

Tracking trails should include:

* animal footprints
* broken branches
* disturbed grass
* claw marks on trees
* fur/feather clues
* scent trail markers
* hidden paths
* sandy tracks
* muddy tracks
* grass movement
* animal sounds

## 3. Dog Companion

After Level 10, the player unlocks a dog companion.

The dog helps with:

* tracking animal footprints
* finding hidden collectibles
* warning about obstacles
* sniffing hidden supplies
* revealing buried treasures
* helping during animal chase missions

The dog must not attack animals. It is a tracking and companion dog.

## 4. Human Encounters

From Level 13 onward, introduce people/NPCs the player can meet.

NPCs can include:

* park guide
* village builder
* wildlife ranger
* market trader
* old storyteller
* carpenter
* tracker
* conservation officer

NPCs should give missions, advice, resources, and story clues.

# Tracking Trail Design Rules

Tracking trails must visually differ from normal running tracks.

Use:

* footprints
* paw prints
* hoofprints
* broken branches
* moving grass
* scent glow trail
* animal sound cues
* subtle camera focus
* dog bark alerts
* trail clues

Avoid:

* plain straight tracks
* repeated water puddles
* empty green roads
* boring flat paths

---

# Animal Chase Rules

Animal chase sections should be exciting but respectful.

The player should:

* follow the animal
* keep it in sight
* avoid obstacles
* collect clues
* photograph/tag/observe it safely

The player should not:

* attack animals
* kill animals
* trap animals harshly
* use weapons

Use wording like:

```text
Track
Observe
Photograph
Rescue
Follow
Protect
Record
```

Do not use wording like:

```text
Kill
Shoot
Slaughter
Hunt down
Attack
```
